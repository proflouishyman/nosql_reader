Oct. 4th, 1904.

Mr. C. W. Galloway., Supt.,
Baltimore, Md.

Dear Sir:
I return herewith a letter of W. E. Cavey, R. F. of relative to Wm. Stanecker's claim for benefits from April to Aug., 03, received with your letter of Sept. 30th, 1904. The Relief Dept. has paid Stanecker benefits from Jan. 19th to May 31st, 1903, inclusive. Mr. Tearney, who had charge of the case reported that Stanecker was able to resume duty on Sept. 1st, 1903, and checks in payment of benefits from June 1st to Aug. 31st, 1903, were sent to the examiner to be delivered to the member. After the releases were read to Stanecker he refused to sign the same or accept the checks. The case was closed as of Aug. 31st, 1903, and the checks last referred to are held in this office. Claim Agent Shaw had the case in hand last spring, but I do not know how he disposed of it.

Very respectfully,

SUMMARY:
This document is an official letter dated October 4th, 1904, addressed to Mr. C.W. Galloway, Superintendent in Baltimore, Maryland. The letter discusses the claim for benefits of Wm. Stanecker, providing a summary of benefit payments made from January 19, 1903, to May 31, 1903, and arrangements made for additional payments for June to August 1903. It notes that Stanecker refused to sign the required releases and declined the checks, leading to the closure of the case as of August 31, 1903. The disposition of some related checks is also described.
