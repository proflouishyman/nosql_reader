Charley Harris
May 31st, 1941.
Dr. O. Carroll Lockard,
2925 N. Charles Street,
Baltimore, Md.
Dear Doctor:-
The case of Charley Harris against the Baltimore and Ohio, which was recently set for trial at Huntington, W. Va., and continued, has been set for trial at Huntington, on June 6th. Our General Claim Agent has requested your presence and has suggested that you take with you the x-rays which were made in this case, or at least a copy of the findings of Dr. Walton. He feels that you could testify that in the course of the general examination x-rays were requested, and were returned with a negative interpretation and that you personally examined the x-rays and agreed with the interpretation, assuming that that is your opinion.
I presume you will arrange to go on the Chesapeake and Ohio, which will arrive at Huntington about 11 A. M.
Very truly yours,
Medical & Surgical Director.
FN/J
Copy to Mr. F. C. Garrott
Your 46-E.

SUMMARY:
The letter, dated May 31, 1941, is addressed to Dr. O. Carroll Lockard regarding the case of Charley Harris versus the Baltimore and Ohio railroad company. The letter discusses the upcoming trial, requests Dr. Lockard's presence, and asks him to bring x-rays or findings from Dr. Walton relevant to the case. The letter confirms the court date, the anticipated travel arrangements, and mentions Dr. Lockard may be asked to testify regarding his interpretation of the x-rays.
