May 26, 1906.

Yardmaster Sellman:

I am in receipt of bill of Hoffman Hospital, amounting to $30.00, for hospital charges between April 3 and 30, 1906, against one Larry Vannette, which you have approved.

We do not appear to have any record of the membership of such a person, but presume the bill is intended to be against Larry Vannette, your 17 Mo. 254. If this is so, please ascertain whether or not we are liable for any charges prior to April 3, 1906, forwarding bill therefore to this office with your report thereon.

In the future, when you approve the payment of bills for hospital charges against members of this Department, please show the correct name of the person against whom the bill is rendered.

Very respectfully,

Superintendent.

A letter dated May 26, 1906, from a superintendent to Yardmaster Sellman discusses the receipt of a $30 hospital bill from Hoffman Hospital for Larry Vannette. The superintendent requests verification regarding Vannette’s membership and liability for charges prior to April 3, 1906. It also requests clarification for future approvals to include the correct person's name on such bills.
