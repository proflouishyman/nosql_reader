205699
HGS:

July 3rd, 1919.

Mr. Wm. H. Parker,
Supreme Medical Director,
The Order of the Golden Seal,
Richmond, Va.

Dear Sir -

Answering your inquiry of the 25th ulto. regarding Ilario Panetta, Keyser, W. Va.

This man is employed as a Car Repairer, and sustained strained muscles of the right shoulder and side of neck, which it is claimed was the result of carrying rails on his shoulder. He reported disabled on April 25th and the Medical Examiner reported that Panetta was not disabled account of the alleged injury, but that he was suffering from acute bronchitis and nasal obstruction. He was operated upon at the Columbus Hospital, New York City for deviation of the nasal septum. This Department has already paid him benefits to include June 24th, and so far as the records show, he is still totally disabled.

Very respectfully,

SUMMARY:
This is a letter dated July 3rd, 1919, addressed to Wm. H. Parker, Supreme Medical Director of The Order of the Golden Seal in Richmond, VA. The letter responds to an inquiry about Ilario Panetta of Keyser, W. Va., who is employed as a Car Repairer. Panetta reported an injury from carrying rails, but was found by a medical examiner to be suffering from bronchitis and nasal obstruction, for which he underwent surgery. He has been paid benefits up to June 24th and is still considered totally disabled.
