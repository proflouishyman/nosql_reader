EMPLOYMENT AND RECORD BUREAU

Baltimore - Sept. 30th, 1920.
File 106309

Mr. W. J. Dudley:-

Referring to yours of September 27th, 1920, relative to re-employment of Thomas B. Freeland, July 28th, 1920, as Car Repairer, M P Department, Cumberland Division, who was previously dismissed from the service March 3rd, 1920, while employed in same department.

In view of the National Agreement with Mechanical Crafts, we cannot at this time, take up the question of securing the Operating Vice President's authority, covering Mr. Freeland's re-employment.

C. A. Duvall -

A letter from the Employment and Record Bureau in Baltimore, dated September 30, 1920, addresses Mr. W. J. Dudley's inquiry about the re-employment of Thomas B. Freeland as a Car Repairer. The letter notes that Freeland was previously dismissed on March 3, 1920, and re-employment cannot proceed at this time, due to agreements with Mechanical Crafts and the need for further authority.
